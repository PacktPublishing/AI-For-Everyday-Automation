# Content on Demand

Write once, publish everywhere. One source becomes multiple formats, distributed from one conversation.

## The Repurposing Chain

### Step 1: Source Material
Gather from: Drive (meeting packets, documents, research briefs), Gmail (email threads), or user-provided notes. Ask for key message (one sentence) and tone guidance.

### Step 2: Generate All Formats
From single source, produce all requested formats:
1. **LINKEDIN POST** (150-200 words): Hook first line. End with question/CTA.
2. **INTERNAL ANNOUNCEMENT** (100-150 words): Casual, celebratory.
3. **CLIENT EMAIL** (200-250 words): Professional, warm, client impact focus.
4. **PRESENTATION BULLETS** (5-7): One line each, lead with impact.
5. **NEWSLETTER PARAGRAPH** (80-100 words): Third person, factual.

Facts identical across all. Tone adapted per audience.

### Step 3: Distribute
After approval: send emails via Gmail, save to Content folder in Drive.

## Style Bank
When user has a Style Bank folder in Drive, read examples before generating. Match tone and hooks from examples.

## Templates

### Thought Leadership
Read from Drive. Hook + 2-3 paragraphs insight + closing. 150-250 words. Reference Style Bank.

### Multi-Audience Project Update
Read meeting packet + task data. Three versions: team (casual), stakeholders (milestones), leadership (3 bullets).

### Launch Announcement
External (200w), internal (100w), press-friendly (150w, third person), social teaser (50w max).

### Newsletter Section
Read month's packets + completed tasks. 200-300 words. Save to Newsletter Drafts.

## When to Write from Scratch
Flag: personal essays, crisis communications, relationship-critical messages.
